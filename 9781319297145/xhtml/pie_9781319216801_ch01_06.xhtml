Active learning: concept mapping exercises

A concept map, often organized around a question, is designed to help you think about the relationships between concepts. First think of concepts and terms that address the question and the relationships among them. Then create a diagram to show these relationships. In the diagram, draw a circle or box around each concept or term. Represent the relationships between concepts with lines that connect the concepts. Each line should have an arrow, showing the direction of the relationship. For each connecting line, add a label consisting of a few words that explains the relationship between the concepts. Include as many connections as you can.

It is important to recognize that there is not one, single correct map for a set of concepts; the same concepts may be organized in different ways.
