Comprehension questions

Section 25.1

  1. What is a Mendelian population? How is the gene pool of a Mendelian population usually described?

Section 25.2

  1. What are the predictions given by the Hardy–Weinberg law?
  2. What assumptions must be met for a population to be in Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium?
  3. What is random mating?
  4. Give the genotypic frequencies expected under the Hardy–Weinberg law for (a) an autosomal locus with three alleles and (b) an X-linked locus with two alleles.

  1. Define inbreeding and briefly describe its effects on a population.

Section 25.4

  1. What determines the allelic frequencies at mutational equilibrium?
  2. What factors affect the magnitude of change in allelic frequencies due to migration?
  3. Define genetic drift, and give three ways in which it can arise. What effect does genetic drift have on a population?
  4. What is effective population size? How does it affect the amount of genetic drift?
  5. Define natural selection and fitness.
  6. Briefly describe the differences between directional selection, overdominance, and underdominance. Describe the effect of each type of selection on the allelic frequencies of a population.
  7. What factors affect the rate of change in allelic frequency due to natural selection?
  8. Compare and contrast the effects of mutation, migration, genetic drift, and natural selection on genetic variation within populations and on genetic divergence between populations.

Bill, who is raising cockroaches in his dorm room, finds that the frequency of the gene for curved wings in his cockroach population is 0.6.   5. *A population of water snakes is found on an island in Lake Erie. After this migration, 20% of the island population consists of snakes that came from the mainland.
    1. If both the mainland population and the island population are assumed to be in Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium for the alleles that affect banding, what is the frequency of the allele for bands on the island and on the mainland before migration?
    2. After migration has taken place, what is the frequency of the allele for the banded phenotype on the island?

Thus, there is little gene flow between populations. Furthermore, each population is small in size and was founded by a small number of pikas.

    A group of population geneticists proposes to study the amount of genetic variation in a series of pika populations and to compare the allelic frequencies in different populations.
